DESIGNING FOR COMMUNITY


INTRODUCTION



In this project, my goal is to design technology to help with community-building at Mount Holyoke. I will speak to some Mount Holyoke students to get a sense of their experiences with making and maintaining friends on campus and design a tool to tackle one aspect of the problems they have encountered during their time on campus so far. The tool I will be designing will be a mobile app that will aid in helping that specific aspect.

INTERVIEWING MOUNT HOLYOKE STUDENTS


In order to get input from 3-5 MHC students, I decided to create a list of questions I aim to ask to guide me and slowly ease into the answers they provide and follow in that direction. I incorporated the semi-structured interview technique. After about 15 minutes, I had my questions ready.







QUESTIONS I ASKED


• How do you typically initiate or maintain friendships on campus?
• Are there any specific barriers you've encountered when trying to connect with other students?
• Have you noticed any patterns or trends in terms of how students socialize or form friendships at Mount Holyoke?
• How do you think technology could be used to improve the social experience for students at Mount Holyoke?
• Can you tell me about your experiences with making friends or building community on campus?

IDENTIFICATION OF THE PROBLEM



I got feedback from all the MHC students I spoke to concerning the problems they faced with how they perceived, initiated, and maintained friendships. I decided to get the perspectives of two racially different groups.
This is what I gathered:
1. Many MHC students are in cliques, sports teams, or clubs. They are close knitted so in order to be a part of that clique you’d have to join them(sport or club).
2. There are different people with different stories and backgrounds and that plays a huge role in the situation.
3. Taking a class with someone does not mean you will be friends after that class.
4. Most people maintain their friendships by texting and meeting up for lunch/dinner in blanch to catch up.
5. Orientation is where people had their first initiation of friendships, but most did not last due to realization that they are no compatible(turkey drop).
6. If you do not have a friend group (clique) after a week or two in your first year, you will usually be alone most times.





PRECISE DESCRIPTION OF THE PROBLEM



There are many people who come from different backgrounds and so they relate to others differently. Once you come into college, you usually try to initialize friendships from orientation, classes, and dorms but sometimes, soon after you realize that you have no common interests except for that one thing which was a class you had together or that dorm you lived in. This makes it hard to relate really well with others, so you tend to find people who share similar interests and backgrounds (maybe originate from the same culture/country) to hang out with and you eventually stay with that group because you are comfortable.

5 DIFFERENT SOLUTIONS


Overall, the interface I am going for is the mobile interface because as college students, our phones are with us 24/7. The aspect of the problem of finding people with similar interests and background begins by asking “how to find them when you arrive on campus”. Even though there is orientation, there is little to no activity after orientation to help student find their spaces where they can interact freely to make them feel at home because they don’t have those similarities and experiences.

• Background-matching: this app would be a mobile app that aims to connect people(mostly first years) with a common background and experience. There will be filters to decide on how to you want to look for other profiles(by age, place of birth, etc.)

• Similar interest hubs: this app will focus on allowing MHC students who do not have cliques or friends yet and long to have friends with similar interests, experiences and background text and plan activities together.

• FriendFinding app(Bump): this app will be like a dating app but for friends. You can create your profile and fill the form with your details and swipe to find a match. You can find more about people on their profiles so far as they are on the app. The more you swipe and bump, the algorithm will recognize and give you more people based on those you bumped with.

• StudyBuddy app : this app will help bring people together who are doing the same major. This is a great idea because if you are doing the same major, you are likely to see the person most days when you have class till you graduate and with that you can express similar interests and take it from there. You have a study buddy and someone you connect with due to shared experiences.

• DormBuddies : this app will help bring people who are in the same building with similar interests together. This helps because once you have someone in your dorm who is like you in the sense that you are compatible, it makes everything more fun.







WHAT I HAVE CHOSEN TO CREATE



After brainstorming on my solutions, I decided to create the Friend Finding App(Bump) where you can find people, like a dating app and see if you are compatible enough. As time goes on, you can find more people and you will be able to expand your friend group. With this, you can swipe and see pictures or ultimately look at profiles and what other people have on theirs which attracts you to bump with them.

An app that I also really liked was study buddy app. With this app, you can start out as acquaintances from class because you are majoring in the same subject and as time goes on, it can build. You can find people with similar background/experience as you and it will make the friendship stronger, but I did not implement this.
