SEEKING AND INTEGRATING FEEDBACK


INTRODUCTION



In this project, I will be evaluating a prototype that I made for a prior project and then use the feedback I receive from that evaluation to improve the project. The prior prototype I have chosen to evaluate and receive feedback from is the Thirsty Minds prototype. I will be using a discount method(Cognitive walkthrough) to see how easy/hard it is to learn what has been given and also use a survey to collect informative data from the users of the prototype. This will help to make the prototype better than it is now. The target audience that I used for the prior project was Current MHC Students and I intend to stick with that audience.

To interact with the prototype, click here.








FEEDBACK I WOULD LIKE TO COLLECT


The first feedback I would like to collect will have to do with how easy or difficult it was to learn how the pages worked and how to move around and get what you want to get. Specifically, how many steps it took to get an action done(example: how were you able to navigate the pages to get to the cold drinks menu?) so a cognitive walkthrough would help with this to access the learnability and usability of the prototype. The second feedback I would like to collect will have to do with the aesthetics and enjoyment and mental effort or ease of how the user was able to go about interacting with the prototype. So, for this I would construct a survey to ask some questions about the prototype. A survey is not expensive and once the questions are structured right, it will help gather a lot of information about the prototype.

For the cognitive walkthrough, I am outlining the steps I want the user to take to get to a specific point.
TASK
1. Start Point: Click on the play button at the top right corner.
• You are on the first screen of Thirsty Minds website.
2. Navigate to the homepage.
3. Look for the drop-down menu called Resources and click on “Menu”.
4. Click on the “Menu” again to reveal the menu options.
5. Look for a menu option that points you to the pastries section.
6. Scroll through and navigate back to the homepage.
7. Repeat the steps from the home page until you get to the order online page.
8. Locate the yelp button at the bottom and click on it.
9. Exit Point:
• Exit the prototype by clicking on the close button.

For the survey, some questions I came up with were:
Mental Ease Questions:
1. How mentally demanding did you find interacting with the prototype?
2. Did you encounter any difficulties or challenges while using the prototype?
3. Were there any aspects of the prototype that required you to really think about what you had to click?

Usability Questions:
1. How was navigating through the prototype like for you?
• On the scale of very easy to very difficult
2. Were you able to view a section from the menu page?
3. Were you able to complete the task given?

Aesthetics and Enjoyment:
1. What is your initial feeling upon looking at the prototype?
2. Did you enjoy using the prototype?
• On a scale of not at all to very much
3. How engaging was the prototype's design?
• On a scale of not engaging at all to very engaging
4. How did the color scheme contribute to your experience?
Finally, I asked an open-ended question:
1. If you could change anything about the prototype, what would it be?

IMPROVEMENTS TO THE PROTOTYPE


After looking at the feedback from the users, I decided that on areas I wanted to improve:
1. An area I wanted to work on and improve would be the dropdown menu since it gave a lot of users trouble because you had to click on it twice before it took you to where you wanted to go which disrupted the mental model of the users so there wasn’t an ease but a mental effort on the part of the users. This is not the typical behavior of a drop-down menu where you click on an option. I would like to change it to buttons. Since I took the loyalty program idea out, there can be enough space for all buttons to be on the page. This would help with the usability and learnability of the prototype, and it will be easier to move between pages since it is just a click away.

This is how the buttons look.

2. Another area I would like to improve is the spacing between the menu options in a particular category. The more legible it is, the more it will make it easier and more enjoyable to use. So, I decided to make it more spaced and make the main components bold and bigger to distinguish it from the actual menu items with prices and I think this would help with the aesthetics.


3. I added more pictures to make it more vibrant. I do believe pictures draw people to a particular restaurant and makes it easy for them to identify what they want. I added a bit of green outline to add a bit more color to make the button vibrant! Below you can see that once the button is clicked, it completely changes color bounces up.

CONCLUSION


In the end I made 3 really impactful changes to my prototype. I replaced the dropdown menu with buttons instead, I added more pictures and colors to the buttons to make it more aesthetically pleasing and also added a bit more space between the options in a specific category of the menu to make it more legible. All these contribute to the goal I had in the beginning with the feedback I wanted in terms of usability, mental ease or effort and aesthetics and enjoyment. I think the changes I made really improved the project. I did have some trouble with the surveys and the cognitive walkthrough.
I struggled a bit with coming up with the perfect task and walkthrough so it will be easy to follow. I also struggled a bit with the questions I wanted to ask for the survey concerning the prototype. This is because I wanted to get very informative feedback and usually surveys do not give you that, but I was able to structure it so that I could get some appropriate feedback.
I think an interview would have been the best user centric approach to go with because I can get a lot of information and understand where the user is coming from, but I did not get the time to meet people and that is a tradeoff I had to do with the survey. In the end, I accomplished the task of getting feedback and improve on the prototype.
