DESIGNING FOR OTHER PEOPLE



INTRODUCTION


For this project, the issues that came up were time management, procrastination, not knowing what to prioritize, getting a daily devotion guide which helps the person have their quiet time and learning how to play the guitar. Of these I decided to talk more about time management/procrastination /not knowing what to prioritize. I decided to formulate ideas that dealt with these issues to some extent. I ended with ideas centered on minimizing such issues. Each app has its special features depending on what the user prefers, and they all have an engaging interface which would attract the user to use that app more.





COLLECTION OF INFORMATION


In order to get a sense of where to start from, I came up with some questions to guide my process and this took about 20 minutes. After getting my questions, I now had to think of the group of people I wanted to ask. I decided to ask those with the same/similar age(20-21) and those in the same major(computer science). I had a semi-structured interview with those I met with in person because as I was asking the questions, I leaned into the answers they seemed most passionate about and kept the conversation going till I had my information.

Questions
1. What are the main challenges or difficulties they face in their daily lives?
2. Are there any specific tasks that makes the frustrated while doing?
3. What are their preferences when it comes to using technology?
4. Are there specific things they hope to achieve through the use of technology?
5. What applications/software do they use regularly?

IDEAS WHICH COULD BE USED TO ADDRESS THE PROBLEMS



Idea 1 – DaFocus

DaFocus is an app that helps you make a list of things you need to get done and notifies you when something needs to be done. There is an algorithm that looks at the time of the day and then takes into consideration what should be a priority and puts that at the top of your list. It will not allow you to complete/ check off other tasks unless you complete/check off the one that is a priority. So, for a day, if you are not able to complete your tasks at the designated time, you will not be able to make a list for the next day until you complete the previous tasks.

To what extent does it address the persona’s challenges
I think in terms of having priority of tasks, this one is really good at it as it helps you go systematically on the tasks and specifically pushes you to finish a task at the top of the list before moving on so a lot can be done in a day. The downside is that you cannot make new tasks for the next day if you have not completed the ones from the day before.



Idea 2 – TuneOut


This is an app that tracks how much you use an app and how much time you spend on it especially social media apps. You will need to manually fill out the calendar to grasp your schedule and add the apps that you know make you unproductive like TikTok and Instagram. After you have filled out the questionnaire about you, the app will be activated. Once it gets a log of the time spend on the app, it will automatically close the app and open up a priority(eg: display on the screen – Homework time/reading time/take shower, etc.) and it will not allow you to just dismiss the message until after some time has passed. The times in the app are from 30 minutes upwards.

To what extent does it address the persona’s challenges
I think since my persona says Instagram and TikTok have them in a choke hold, this would be the perfect app to at least help them literally tune out and do something productive as suggested to them through the questionnaire they answered concerning tasks and others. A downside might be that they could just move to their laptops/other devices that do not have the app.



Idea 3 – PTMF


Priority Time Managed Focus(PTMF) is an app that mimics a game. It is meant to make scheduling meetings, classes, work etc. very fun. The gamer gets points for how efficiently they completed a priority task, managed their time best and finished doing the suggested tasks by the app. It is a daily event so allows the user to feel proud for accomplishing something. It is a daily event so allows the user to feel proud for accomplishing something. It is a collaborative app where you can invite friends and interact with them through the completion of tasks.

To what extent does it address the persona’s challenges
This app aims to make completing tasks very fun with its unique and exciting interface. Who wouldn’t want to play a game that helps them complete their task? This is for those who are unproductive during the mornings as It sets a mood for being ahead of the game even before midday. It also helps because you are interacting with friends who have the app as well, so you tend to push it other to reach your goal for the day.



Idea 4 – DailyBible


This is an app that helps you do your devotion daily with focused prayer points and has a scheduled time it will alert you to pray. If you don’t know where to start, this app has got you!

To what extent does it address the persona’s challenges
In terms of devotion and not having the time to pray or what to pray about, this app addresses the issue pretty well as it guides the person through a whole devotion in a matter of minutes, so they never have to wonder about where to start. In terms of other challenges, this does not help as much.



Idea 5 – Inspire!


This is an app designed to have daily prompts for goal setting and also a space for a reflection when the day is over/ goal has been reached. There Is always going to be a motivational quote every day when you open the app. Of course, you may forget to open the app so it will be designed to pop up during the mornings so you can tap it to engage with it. This helps with visualizing your goals and then achieving them so by the end of the day you would’ve used you time wisely and would have done everything you wanted to do. The only thing is once you put in your goals and how to achieve them. You can’t modify it so once you tick all the boxes, the app will close automatically until the next day.

To what extent does it address the persona’s challenges
This app I think is a great one because it combats the challenge of being unproductive during the mornings after waking up. Once you are able to sign in and get the app going with your information, it tunes your mind in a way to think about what you want to achieve in the day and how you can go about it, allowing you to journal along the way and closes once you have achieved your goal.





CONCLUSION



The ideas I came up with all have unique features to them which distinguish them from others. I delved deeper into the aspects of task prioritization and time management. I also explored various UI/UX that would help my persona/interviewees accomplish their goals and finally came up with 5 ideas to which I acknowledge are a good start for combating the problems faced by my interviewees and persona.
